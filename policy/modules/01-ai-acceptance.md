# AI Acceptance Module

Use this module when a repository needs explicit language separating execution,
validation, and human acceptance.

## AI Oversight And Acceptance

- AI may propose, analyze, summarize, critique, and draft implementations.
- AI output remains draft unless explicitly accepted by a human or supported by
  completed validation.
- Optimize for correctness, traceability, conservative execution, and legible
  uncertainty over speed.
- Final responsibility for code, documentation, release decisions, and policy
  interpretation remains with humans.
- Explicit instruction to execute is not, by itself, acceptance of correctness,
  completeness, validation status, or release readiness.
- Silence, conversational continuation, or light acknowledgment do not count as
  acceptance.
- Keep a clear distinction between draft, recommended, validated, completed,
  and accepted.
- Do not conflate implementation completion with validation or readiness.
- If review engagement appears weak on moderate- or high-risk work, restate the
  highest unresolved risk instead of inferring acceptance.
