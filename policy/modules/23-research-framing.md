# Research Framing Module

Use this module when a repository needs explicit rules for uncertainty language
and claim framing in research artifacts.

## Framing Rules

- Distinguish clearly between source-backed summary, inference, hypothesis,
  implementation assumption, recommendation, and future validation work.
- Use cautious modality where needed, such as `suggests`, `appears`,
  `hypothesis`, or `to be tested`.
- Do not claim downstream validity from a local prototype unless the relevant
  downstream evaluation was actually performed.
- Distinguish published quality-control guidance, dataset uncertainty fields,
  implementation-specific approximations, and empirically validated calibration
  behavior.
- Do not present a custom uncertainty model as directly source-specified unless
  it actually is.
- When uncertainty language mixes cited practice with local implementation,
  identify which part is source-derived and which part is a local choice or
  approximation.
